Don't rent a boat. Hire the guy who grew up here.

A rental gives you a hull, a map, and a full day of guessing. Key Largo's water is a maze of flats, channels, and reef lines that punishes guesswork, and the fish do not wait around while you figure it out.

Captain Brian puts you on his boat, on his spots, on his tide. One captain, one boat, and five decades of local knowledge working for you by the day or by the week. You fish, swim, snorkel, eat, and explore. He handles everything else.

One captain. Every hour of the water.

Most charters sell you a half-day block. Brian builds trips around when your target actually bites, from first light to well past midnight.

Two very different sunsets. Barhop the waterfront restaurants of the Upper Keys by boat and eat where the docks are the front door. Or stake out a flat as the light drops and hook into a shark in water shallow enough to see the whole fight.

The captain

Son of fishing royalty. Raised on this water.

Captain Brian Epstein grew up in the Florida Keys as the son of Bob Epstein, the Miami Herald's fishing editor, a photojournalist and author who spent a lifetime writing the book on Keys fishing. Brian has been fishing and exploring these waters since 1976, and it shows in every spot he runs to without checking a chart.

He is a USCG 100 Ton Master captain and a proven tournament captain and decorated tournament angler. Alongside Captain Key Largo, he co-runs the Poppa Wahoo Collective, the family's Islamorada sportfishing fleet founded in tribute to his father.
